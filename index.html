Day 1 - Monday

Arasta Bazaar

Arasta Bazaar

Historic bazaar located behind the Blue Mosque, known for shops selling carpets, crafts and souvenirs.

Tip: Great place to buy authentic Turkish souvenirs and handicrafts.

Turkish breakfast (kahvaltı)

Breakfast

Start your day at one of the local cafes near Arasta Bazaar. Try traditional Turkish breakfast (kahvaltı) which includes cheese, olives, eggs, honey, jam and fresh bread.

Blue Mosque (Sultanahmet Camii)

Blue Mosque (Sultanahmet Camii)

One of the most impressive mosques in Istanbul, known for its six minarets and beautiful blue tiles in the interior.

Impressive interior and ambiance with more than 20,000 hand-painted ceramic tiles.

Note: Modest dress required (covered shoulders and knees), head scarf recommended for women.

Hagia Sophia (Ayasofya-i Kebir Camii)

Hagia Sophia (Ayasofya-i Kebir Camii)

Former Byzantine church, later converted to a mosque, museum, and then back to a mosque. An architectural marvel nearly 1,500 years old.

Historical significance and visual impact - a blend of Christian and Islamic art and architecture.

Tip: Pay attention to the enormous dome and mosaics dating from different periods.

Eyüp Sultan Mosque

Eyüp Sultan Mosque and Complex

One of the holiest places in Istanbul, an important pilgrimage site for Muslims.

Peaceful atmosphere, local spirit away from tourist crowds

The complex includes a hamam, fountains and an Islamic cemetery with artistically valuable tombstones.
